725 5 Tools and Datasets for Building EXHYTE

726                 Workflows

727  The preceding sections surveyed strategies for each EXHYTE stage. Here we comple-

728  ment that view with the practical tools, APIs, and datasets that enable those strategies

729  to operate. We group resources by their role in the cycle and note where they most

730  naturally plug into EXHYTE.

731 5.1 Core tools and APIs

732  Literature & data access (E2). For scientific publications and metadata, commonly

733  used resources include Semantic Scholar[78], PubMed[79], arXiv[80], OpenAlex[81],

734  NASA ADS[82]. Chemical and materials data are typically sourced from PubChem[83],

735  Materials Project[40], and the Amazon EC2 API[84].

736  Text parsing, extraction & normalization (E3). A variety of tools are

737  employed for text parsing, extraction and normalization. PDF and document con-

738  tent are commonly processed using PyPDF2[85], SciPDF[86], pymupdf4llm[87], and

739  S2ORC-doc2json[88], while RecursiveCharacterTextSplitter[89] is used for doc-

740  ument chunking. HTML content is parsed with BeautifulSoup[90]. For entity

741   extraction and normalization, tools such as BLINK[91], the NER[92] tool, PL-Marker[93],

742  SciCo[94], and ScispaCy[95] are frequently applied.

743  Vector representations, similarity search & retrieval (E2/E3). A range

744  of embedding generation methods are used to represent text, structured data,

745  molecular properties, and knowledge graph nodes. Text embeddings are com-

746  monly produced using models such as Text-Embedding-3 (OpenAI)[96], BERT[97],

747  SciBERT[98], SentenceBERT[99] (all-MiniLM-L6-v2), BAAI/bge-large-en-v1.5[100],

748  Google-GenerativeAIEmbeddings[101],  and  jina-embedding-v3[102].  Molecular

749  similarity is computed using measures such as the Tanimoto coefficient[103] based

750  on fingerprints and ChemBERTa[? ] cosine similarity from last hidden state embeddings.

751  For efficient similarity search and retrieval, vector indices and nearest neighbor search

752  libraries such as FAISS[104] are widely used, supporting fast retrieval of semantically

753  or chemically similar items.

754  Cheminformatics and molecular analysis (H2). Several specialized tools such

755  as RDKit[105] for molecular feature calculation, OmegaFold[106] for protein folding

756  prediction, and SynergyFinder 3.0[107] for evaluating drug synergy and additivity

757  from experimental data.

758 5.2 Benchmark datasets and evaluation corpora

759  Drug Discovery, Bioactivity, and Clinical: Several benchmark datasets are

760  widely used in drug discovery and clinical research. DrugBank[22] and KIBA[23] pro-

761  vide molecular and bioactivity data for drug-target interaction prediction, while

762   DAVIS[108] offers drug and protein binding affinity data. Large-scale compound collec-

763  tions such as ZINC[109] (over 230 million purchasable 3D compounds) and ChEMBL[110]

764  (bioactive compounds with properties) are commonly used for training, activity opti-

765   mization, and modification tasks. Protein-ligand docking performance is assessed using

766  CrossDocked2020[111], and clinical evaluation often relies on MIMIC-CDM[112], which

767  contains 2,400 patients with four abdominal conditions including patient histories,

768  physical exam notes, imaging reports, and laboratory results.

769  Materials Science and Chemistry: In materials science, datasets such as MP-20 (a

770  subset of Materials Project[40] with 45,231 inorganic crystalline materials), NOMAD[39]

771  (2.9 million materials), Matbench[113], and JARVIS-DFT[114] (61,541 crystal structures

772  with bandgap values) are commonly used for property prediction and generative mod-

773  eling. OC20-S2EF 2M[115] provides two million catalyst structures for training models

774  such as CatGPT. Additional chemistry-focused benchmarks include TOMATO-Chem[2],

775  for hypothesis rediscovery and ranking from annotated papers, and Superconductor

776  Optimization (SPO)[17], which contains 26,321 superconducting materials with

777  chemical formulas and critical temperatures.

778  Auxiliary Multi-domain Testbeds: Various multi-domain datasets are used for

779  evaluation in NLP, AI reasoning, and behavioral tasks. BUSINESS SHOES[116] evalu-

780  ates binary classification for shoe suitability, and DECEPTIVE REVIEWS[117] contains

781  genuine and fake hotel reviews for deception detection. HEADLINE POPULARITY[118]

782  and TWEET POPULARITY[119] assess popularity prediction of headlines and tweets,

783  respectively, while DREADDIT[120] and PERSUASIVE PAIRS[121] focus on mental

784  stress detection and persuasive argument prediction. Systematic review and reason-

785  ing datasets include RCT Summaries[122], which links randomized controlled trials

786  to PubMed papers, and HypoGen[45], containing approximately 5,500 structured

787  problem-hypothesis pairs from NeurIPS 2023 and ICLR 2024 papers, used for

788  fine-tuning and evaluation.

789 5.3 How these resources connect to EXHYTE

790  E2 (Data Retrieval) is powered by literature and dataset retrieval APIs(Semantic

791  Scholar[78], PubMed[79], arXiv[80], OpenAlex[81], PubChem[83], Amazon EC2[84])

792  and efficient similarity search tools such as FIASS[123], Tanimoto coefficient[103] etc.

793  E3 (Knowledge Assembly) leverages PyPDF2[85], SciPDF[86] for parsing docu-

794  ments, RecursiveCharacterTextSplitter[89] for document chunking, pymupdf4llm[87],

795  S2ORC-doc2json[88] for documents formatting, BLINK[91], NER[92], PL-Marker[93],

796  SciCo[94] for entity extraction and normalization. H2 (Prioritization) draws

797  on similarity metrics (Tanimoto[103], ChemBERTa[124]) and benchmark corpora

798  (e.g., TOMATO-Chem[2], Matbench[113]) to score plausibility and novelty against

799  known neighborhoods. T1–T2 (Test & Refinement) use domain datasets (OC20-

800  S2EF[115], JARVIS-DFT[114], MIMIC-CDM[112], ZINC[109]) to instantiate exe-

801  cutable protocols, measure performance with domain metrics, and iterate using

802  empirical feedback.

803                A typical in silico screening loop begins by validating textual inputs (molecular

804  SMILE string using RDKit[105]) and PyPDF2[85], SciPDF[86], and pymupdf4llm[87]

805  are used for related PDF documents parsing and content extraction sup-

806  ported by structured conversion pipelines such as S2ORC-doc2json[88] and the

807  RecursiveCharacterTextSplitter[89]. Candidate representations are then retrieved

808  through embedding-based similarity search, using FAISS[104] for vector indexing and

809  retrieval, Tanimoto[103] fingerprint similarity or ChemBERTa[124] latent-space cosine

810  similarity for molecular comparison, and text embeddings generated via models such

811  as Text-Embedding-3[101], SciBERT[98], or BAAI/bge-large-en-v1.5[100]. Task-

812  relevant features are extracted and normalized via entity recognition and linking tools

813  including BLINK[91], NER[92], PL-Marker[93], SciCo[94], and ScispaCy[95]. Candi-

814  date hypotheses or molecular designs are subsequently evaluated on domain-specific

815  benchmark datasets—such as Matbench[113] or MP-20[40] providing quantitative feed-

816  back for H2 prioritization and informing iterative refinement under T1–T2. This

817  loop enables EXHYTE systems to continuously integrate retrieval (E2), structured

818  knowledge assembly (E3), and hypothesis ranking (H2) within a unified testing and

819  improvement cycle.
