85 2 The EXHYTE Cycle:A workflow for data-intensive

86                 scientific discovery

87  We derived the EXHYTE cycle from two complementary sources: recent advances

88  in the philosophy of scientific discovery [9–11], which emphasizes iterative and

89  exploratory models of inquiry, and a systematic review of workflows used in state-

90  of-the-art AI–driven research systems. Across these works, we observed a shared

91  structure in which exploration of large knowledge bases leads naturally to hypothesis

92  generation and targeted testing, forming a self-sustaining loop that integrates human

93  reasoning with computational and experimental processes. EXHYTE formalizes this

94  pattern as an explicit, generalizable framework for data-intensive discovery.

95

96                Research typically enters the EXHYTE cycle through a trigger (Fig. 1), for

97  example, an open question, an unexpected observation, an anomaly in data, or the

98  introduction of a new measurement technology. Such triggers define a broad area of

99  inquiry but rarely yield immediately testable predictions. The first stage, EXPLORE,

100  therefore seeks to organize what is known and expose what remains uncertain.

101  EXPLORE aims to assemble and structure knowledge so that knowledge gaps

102  become explicit and actionable. This stage consists of three substages:

103  • E1: Query structuring translates research questions into machine-actionable

104              forms (e.g., keyphrases, controlled vocabularies, domain schemas) to guide retrieval.

105  • E2: Data retrieval collects literature, code, and structured datasets from scientific

106              repositories and APIs according to the structured query plan in E1.

107  • E3: Knowledge assembly normalizes and integrates retrieved data into represen-

108              tations such as embeddings, graphs, or feature tables and summarizes evidence to

109              surface explicit knowledge gaps.

110  The output of EXPLORE is a structured knowledge base and a set of defined knowl-

111  edge gaps that motivate the next stage.

112

113  HYPOTHESIZE transforms knowledge gaps into ideas or specific, testable claims

114  through two substages:

115  • H1: Hypothesis/idea generation formulates candidate mechanisms or inter-

116              ventions using approaches such as literature-grounded reasoning or data-driven

117             strategies.

118  • H2: Hypothesis/idea prioritization evaluates these candidates for novelty, plau-

119             sibility, feasibility and expected impact using quantitative metrics or expert and

120             LLM-based assessments.

121  The outcome of this stage is a prioritized list of hypotheses with clear rationales and

122  success criteria, ready for empirical validation.

123

124   TEST translates hypotheses into executable experiments and closes the feedback loop.

125  • T1: Experimental design specifies objectives, protocols, datasets/materials, and

126             measurable outcomes, yielding executable experimental or computational plans.

127  • T2: Testing and refinement performs in silico, in vitro or in vivo experiments

128              and analyzes the results to confirm, refine, or reject hypotheses.

129  Results from TEST feed back into the cycle through one of the three paths. When

130  the findings generally support the hypothesis but leave important uncertainties, the

131  process proceeds through refinement by adjusting parameters, recording additional

132  variables, or refining the experimental design while maintaining the same mecha-

133  nistic focus. When evidence contradicts the hypothesis, the cycle pivots, preserving

134  the original knowledge gap but exploring alternative explanations or mechanisms in

135  the next iteration. In contrast, when success criteria are fully met and the research

136  question is resolved, the inquiry sunsets, concluding the current line of investigation

137  rather than looping back to further exploration. Together, these outcomes ensure that

138   EXHYTE functions as a self-correcting, evidence-driven process, capable of continuous

139  adaptation while allowing closure when discovery goals are achieved.
